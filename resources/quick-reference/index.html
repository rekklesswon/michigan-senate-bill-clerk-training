    Bill Lifecycle Stages (Quick Version)

    1. Introduction → First Reading, assigned number, referred to committee
    2. Committee → Hearings, amendments, reported back (or dies)
    3. General Orders → Second Reading, debate, amendments, advanced
    4. Third Reading → Five-day rule, final passage vote (20 votes needed)
    5. Immediate Effect → Separate vote (26 votes needed) [optional]
    6. House → Same process in other chamber
    7. Concurrence/Conference → Resolve differences if House amended
    8. Governor → Sign, veto, or do nothing
    9. Enactment → Becomes Public Act

    Committee Actions Cheat Sheet

    Committee Action Meaning Next Step
    Do Pass Approved as written → General Orders
    Do Pass as Amended Approved with changes → General Orders
    Committee Substitute Major rewrite → General Orders
    Refer to Another Committee Send to different committee → Other committee
    Postponed Delayed Stays in committee
    No Action Never voted / voted down Dies in committee

    Floor Motions Cheat Sheet

    Motion Effect Vote Needed
    Advance to Third Reading Move from GO to 3rd Majority
    Table Kill bill (indefinite postpone) Majority
    Postpone Delay to specific time Majority
    Refer/Re-refer Send to committee Majority
    Reconsider Bring back previous vote Majority
    Concur Accept House amendments 20 votes
    Non-Concur Reject House amendments Majority
    Adopt Conference Report Accept compromise 20 votes

    Voting Requirements Table

    Action Votes Required Vote Type
    Pass Bill 20 (majority) Roll call required
    Immediate Effect 26 (2/3) Roll call required
    Adopt Amendment Majority present Voice or roll call
    Concur in Amendments 20 (majority) Usually roll call
    Adopt Conference Report 20 (majority) Usually roll call
    Override Veto 26 (2/3) Roll call required
    Suspend Rules 26 (2/3) Roll call
    Most Procedural Motions Majority present Usually voice

    Five-Day Rule Reminder ⏰

    What: Bill must be available in final form for 5 CALENDAR days before final passage vote

    When it starts: When bill placed on Third Reading calendar

    How to count: 5 full calendar days (includes weekends/holidays)

    Example: - Placed on Third Reading: Monday, March 3 - Count: Tue 3/4, Wed 3/5, Thu 3/6, Fri 3/7, Sat 3/8 - Eligible for vote: Saturday, March 8 (or any day after)

    Exception: Can be waived by 2/3 vote (rare)

    What to Listen For During Sessions

    Introductions:

    ✓ Bill number
    ✓ Sponsor name
    ✓ Subject
    ✓ Committee referral

    Committee Reports:

    ✓ Committee name
    ✓ Bill number
    ✓ Action (do pass, do pass as amended, substitute, refer)
    ✓ Next committee (if multiple referrals)

    General Orders:

    ✓ Bill number
    ✓ Amendments offered (number, sponsor)
    ✓ Vote on each amendment (adopted/defeated)
    ✓ Motion to advance
    ✓ Vote result

    Third Reading:

    ✓ Bill number
    ✓ Vote count (Yeas, Nays, Excused, Not Voting)
    ✓ Passed or defeated
    ✓ Immediate effect vote (separate count)
    ✓ IE granted or denied

    House Messages:

    ✓ Bill number
    ✓ House action (passed as is / with amendments)
    ✓ Senate response (concur/non-concur)
    ✓ Conference committee (if applicable)

    Common Phrases You'll Hear

    Opening: - "The Senate will come to order" - "The Secretary will call the roll" - "A quorum is present"

    Bills: - "Senate Bill [#], sponsored by Senator [name]" - "First reading of the bill" - "The bill is referred to the Committee on [name]" - "The question being on passage of the bill" - "The bill passed" / "The bill failed"

    Amendments: - "I offer Floor Amendment [#]" - "The question being on adoption of the amendment" - "The amendment is adopted" / "The amendment is defeated"

    Motions: - "I move the bill be advanced to Third Reading" - "I move immediate effect" - "I move the Senate concur in the House amendments" - "I move the Senate non-concur" - "Without objection, so ordered"

    Voting: - "All in favor say 'aye.' All opposed say 'no.'" - "The Secretary will call the roll" - "Roll call: Yeas [#], Nays [#], Excused [#], Not Voting [#]" - "Immediate effect is granted" / "Immediate effect is denied"

    Immediate Effect Guide

    Why it matters: Laws normally take effect 90 days after session ends. IE makes them effective immediately.

    Requirements: - Separate vote AFTER passage - Needs 26 votes (2/3) - Always roll call vote

    Possible outcomes: - 26+ votes: IE granted → Law effective when Governor signs - 25 or fewer: IE denied → Law effective 90 days after sine die - Important: Bill still becomes law even if IE denied!

    Bill Clerk Golden Rules

    1. Listen carefully - miss nothing
    2. Code accurately - precision matters
    3. Work quickly - floor moves fast
    4. Ask when unsure - never guess
    5. Track amendments - number them correctly
    6. Count votes carefully - exact numbers required
    7. Know vote thresholds - 20 for passage, 26 for IE
    8. Watch five-day rule - count calendar days
    9. Note House actions - track inter-chamber movement
    10. Stay focused - long sessions test endurance
