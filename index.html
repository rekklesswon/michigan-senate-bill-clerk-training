    Michigan Senate Bill Clerk Training Course

    Welcome! This comprehensive training course prepares you for the Michigan Senate Bill Clerk position through 20 hours of self-paced study covering legislative processes, procedures, and terminology.

    Course Duration

    20 hours over 2 weeks of self-paced study

    What You'll Learn

    Real-time coding of legislative actions during Senate sessions, complete bill lifecycle tracking, and all Michigan-specific procedures and terminology

    Ready to Begin?

    Start with Module 1: Foundations

    üéØ What You'll Learn

    This course provides everything you need to understand Michigan's legislative process and excel as a Bill Clerk:

    • Michigan government structure and Senate operations
    • Complete bill lifecycle from introduction to enactment
    • Committee processes and all 17 standing committees
    • Floor procedures (General Orders and Third Reading)
    • Comprehensive legislative terminology
    • Senate-House interaction and conference committees
    • Real-time coding of legislative actions
    • Interview preparation strategies

    üë®‚Äçüíº What Bill Clerks Do

    Bill Clerks are essential to Michigan Senate operations. Your role would include:

    • Code legislative actions in real-time during Senate sessions
    • Track bills through committee, floor debates, and votes
    • Record amendments, motions, and vote counts accurately
    • Monitor bills moving between Senate and House
    • Maintain precise records for the official Senate Journal
    • Support Senate leadership and operations

    Key Skills Required: - Attention to detail and accuracy - Ability to work quickly under pressure - Strong listening and comprehension skills - Knowledge of legislative procedures and terminology - Professional communication - Focus during long sessions

    üìö Course Structure

    Core Modules (14-18 hours)

    Module 1: Foundations of Michigan Government

    Estimated Time: 2-3 hours

    • Three branches of Michigan government
    • Bicameral legislature (38 Senate, 110 House)
    • Senate leadership positions and roles
    • All 17 standing committees for 2025-2026
    • Federal vs. State differences
    • Visual diagrams and self-check quiz

    Module 2: Bill Lifecycle - How Bills Become Law

    Estimated Time: 3-4 hours

    • Complete bill stages: Introduction through enactment
    • Three readings requirement
    • Five-day rule (5 calendar days before final passage)
    • Committee actions and General Orders
    • Voting requirements (20 for passage, 26 for immediate effect)
    • Governor's options and veto process
    • Real-world timeline examples
    • Flowcharts and self-check quiz

    Module 3: Committee Process and Structure

    Estimated Time: 2-3 hours

    • All 17 Michigan Senate standing committees with jurisdictions
    • Committee roles (chair, vice chairs, members)
    • Committee procedures (hearings, votes, reports)
    • Committee actions Bill Clerks must code
    • Appropriations Committee special case
    • Multiple referrals and special situations
    • Flowcharts and self-check quiz

    Module 4: Senate Floor Procedures

    Estimated Time: 3-4 hours

    • How Senate sessions operate (order of business)
    • General Orders (Committee of the Whole) - amendment stage
    • Floor amendment types and processes
    • Voting procedures (voice vote, roll call, division)
    • Third Reading and final passage
    • Immediate effect votes (2/3 majority required)
    • Common floor motions and parliamentary procedures
    • Real-time coding examples
    • Flowcharts and self-check quiz

    Module 5: Legislative Terminology and Language

    Estimated Time: 2-3 hours

    • Core legislative terms (bill, PA, SB, HB, etc.)
    • Bill status terminology
    • Committee and floor action terms
    • Voting terminology
    • People and roles
    • Common abbreviations
    • Phrases heard during sessions
    • Calendar and fiscal terms
    • Comprehensive self-check quiz

    Module 6: Senate-House Interaction

    Estimated Time: 2 hours

    • Bicameral legislature requirement
    • How bills move between chambers
    • Concurrence process (concur vs. non-concur)
    • Conference Committees (purpose, process, reports)
    • Real-world bill "ping-pong" example
    • Senate vs. House differences
    • Inter-chamber bill flow flowchart
    • Self-check quiz

    Practice Materials (4-6 hours)

    Practice Scenarios: Real-World Coding

    Estimated Time: 2-3 hours

    Eight realistic scenarios including: - Scenario 1: Committee Report - Scenario 2: Floor Amendment (voice vote) - Scenario 3: Advancing to Third Reading - Scenario 4: Third Reading and Final Passage - Scenario 5: Immediate Effect Vote - Scenario 6: House Amendments and Concurrence - Scenario 7: Conference Committee Process - Scenario 8: Adopting Conference Report - Quick Coding Challenge

    Each includes realistic dialogue, trainee questions, and detailed answer keys.

    Terminology Quiz: Test Your Knowledge

    Estimated Time: 1-2 hours

    • 75+ questions covering all modules
    • Multiple choice, fill-in-blank, and scenario-based questions
    • Organized by topic with expandable answers
    • Scoring guide and performance interpretation
    • Identifies areas needing additional review

    Resources (2-3 hours)

    Quick Reference Guide

    Reference tool - 30 minutes

    One-page reference including: - Key numbers (38, 110, 17, 20, 26, 5) - Bill lifecycle stages (condensed) - Committee actions cheat sheet - Floor motions cheat sheet - Voting requirements table - All 17 committees listed - Common terminology glossary - Phrases you'll hear during sessions - Five-day rule calculator

    Tip: Print and laminate for easy reference!

    Interview Preparation Guide

    Estimated Time: 2-3 hours

    Complete interview prep including: - Understanding the Bill Clerk role - Common interview questions with sample answers - How teaching experience transfers to this role - Questions to ask the interviewer - What to bring and how to prepare - Night-before checklist - Day-of-interview checklist - Thank-you email template - Common mistakes to avoid

    üéØ Michigan-Specific Key Facts

    Numbers to Memorize:

    • 38 Senators (4-year terms)
    • 110 Representatives (2-year terms)
    • 17 standing committees (2025-2026 session)
    • 20 votes for passage (majority of 38)
    • 26 votes for immediate effect (2/3 of 38)
    • 5 calendar days (five-day rule)
    • 90 days after session ends (default effective date)

    Important Rules:

    • Three readings required for all bills
    • Five-day rule: Bill must be available 5 calendar days before final passage
    • Immediate effect: Requires 2/3 vote (26 of 38); otherwise law takes effect 90 days after session
    • Identical versions: Both chambers must pass same text before going to Governor
    • Lt. Governor presides as President of Senate
    • No filibuster in Michigan Senate (unlike U.S. Senate)

    The 17 Standing Committees:

    1. Appropriations
    2. Civil Rights, Judiciary, and Public Safety

    4. Education
    5. Elections and Ethics

    10. Housing and Human Services
    11. Labor
    12. Local Government
    13. Natural Resources and Agriculture
    14. Oversight
    15. Regulatory Affairs
    16. Transportation and Infrastructure
    17. Veterans and Emergency Services
