## üë®‚Äçüíº What Bill Clerks Do

Bill Clerks are essential to Michigan Senate operations. Your role would include:

- **Code legislative actions in real-time** during Senate sessions
- Track bills through committee, floor debates, and votes
- Record amendments, motions, and vote counts accurately
- Monitor bills moving between Senate and House
- Maintain precise records for the official Senate Journal
- Support Senate leadership and operations

**Key Skills Required:**
- Attention to detail and accuracy
- Ability to work quickly under pressure
- Strong listening and comprehension skills
- Knowledge of legislative procedures and terminology
- Professional communication
- Focus during long sessions

## üéØ Michigan-Specific Key Facts

### Numbers to Memorize:

- **38 Senators** (4-year terms)
- **110 Representatives** (2-year terms)
- **17 standing committees** (2025-2026 session)
- **20 votes** for passage (majority of 38)
- **26 votes** for immediate effect (2/3 of 38)
- **5 calendar days** (five-day rule)
- **90 days** after session ends (default effective date)

### Important Rules:

- **Three readings required** for all bills
- **Five-day rule:** Bill must be available 5 calendar days before final passage
- **Immediate effect:** Requires 2/3 vote (26 of 38); otherwise law takes effect 90 days after session
- **Identical versions:** Both chambers must pass same text before going to Governor
- **Lt. Governor presides** as President of Senate
- **No filibuster** in Michigan Senate (unlike U.S. Senate)

### The 17 Standing Committees:

1. Appropriations
2. Civil Rights, Judiciary, and Public Safety
3. Economic and Community Development
4. Education
5. Elections and Ethics
6. Energy and Environment

9. Health Policy
