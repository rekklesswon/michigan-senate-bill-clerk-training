    Section 1: Basic Legislative Terms (10 questions)

    Question 1

    What is the difference between a bill and a Public Act?

    Answer A **bill** is a proposed law that has not yet been enacted. A **Public Act (PA)** is a bill that has passed both chambers and been signed into law by the Governor. Bills become Public Acts when enacted.

    Question 2

    What does "SB 234" indicate?

    Answer **SB** means Senate Bill, indicating this bill originated in the Michigan Senate (not the House). The number 234 is the sequential number assigned when introduced.

    Question 3

    What is a sponsor?

    Answer The **sponsor** is the Senator who introduces a bill and serves as its primary author and advocate.

    Question 4

    True or False: A resolution has the same force of law as a bill.

    Answer **False.** Resolutions are legislative measures that do NOT have the force of law. They are typically ceremonial or procedural. Only bills that become Public Acts are actual laws.

    Question 5

    What does MCL stand for?

    Answer **Michigan Compiled Law** - the collection of Michigan state statutes already on the books.

    Question 6

    What is the difference between a co-sponsor and a sponsor?

    Answer The **sponsor** introduces the bill and is the primary author. **Co-sponsors** are other Senators who add their names in support but did not introduce the bill.

    Question 7

    How many senators are in the Michigan Senate?

    Answer **38 Senators**

    Question 8

    How long is a Senator's term?

    Answer **4 years**

    Question 9

    Who presides over the Michigan Senate?

    Answer The **Lieutenant Governor** serves as President of the Senate and presides over sessions.

    Question 10

    What is a bicameral legislature?

    Answer A legislature with **two chambers** (Senate and House). Michigan has 38 Senators and 110 Representatives.

    Section 2: Bill Status and Process (10 questions)

    Question 11

    What is "First Reading"?

    Answer **First Reading** is when a bill is introduced, given a number, and read by title on the Senate floor. The bill is then referred to committee.

    Question 12

    What does "referred to committee" mean?

    Answer The bill has been assigned to a committee for review, hearings, and possible amendments before returning to the full Senate.

    Question 13

    What is General Orders?

    Answer **General Orders** (also called Committee of the Whole or Second Reading) is the stage where the full Senate debates bills and considers amendments in a less formal setting than Third Reading.

    Question 14

    What is Third Reading?

    Answer **Third Reading** is the final, formal stage where the Senate votes on passage of a bill. It requires a roll call vote and is subject to the five-day rule.

    Question 15

    What is the five-day rule?

    Answer A bill must be available in its final form for **5 calendar days** (not session days) after being placed on Third Reading before the Senate can vote on final passage.

    Question 16

    What does "enrolled" mean?

    Answer A bill is **enrolled** when it has passed both chambers in identical form and is being officially prepared to send to the Governor.

    Question 17

    What does it mean when a bill is "tabled"?

    Answer **Tabled** means postponed indefinitely, which usually kills the bill as it rarely comes back for consideration.

    Question 18

    What is the difference between "postponed" and "tabled"?

    Answer **Postponed** means delayed to a specific time (the bill will be reconsidered). **Tabled** means postponed indefinitely (bill usually dies).

    Question 19

    If a bill is "defeated," what does that mean?

    Answer The bill did not receive enough votes to pass (fewer than 20 votes in the Senate). The bill is dead unless a motion to reconsider is made.

    Question 20

    What does "vetoed" mean?

    Answer The Governor rejected the bill and returned it to the Legislature. The Legislature can attempt to override the veto with a 2/3 vote in both chambers.

    Section 3: Committee Actions (8 questions)

    Question 21

    How many standing committees does the Michigan Senate have?

    Answer **17 standing committees** for the 2025-2026 session.

    Question 22

    Which committee reviews all bills with fiscal impact?

    Answer The **Appropriations Committee** reviews all bills that spend state money.

    Question 23

    What does "reported with recommendation 'do pass'" mean?

    Answer The committee approves the bill **as written** with no changes and sends it to General Orders.

    Question 24

    What does "reported with recommendation 'do pass as amended'" mean?

    Answer The committee made **amendments** (changes) and approves the amended version for General Orders.

    Question 25

    What is a committee substitute?

    Answer A **committee substitute** is when the committee significantly rewrites a bill, replacing the original with a new version.

    Question 26

    If a committee "refers a bill to another committee," what happens?

    Answer The bill is sent to a different committee for additional review (common pattern: policy committee Appropriations).

    Question 27

    What is the most common way bills die?

    Answer **Dying in committee** through inaction. The committee chair never schedules a hearing, or the committee votes it down. Approximately 60-70% of bills die in committee.

    Question 28

    Who has the most power in a committee?

    Answer The **committee chair** because they decide which bills get hearings and control the committee agenda.

    Section 4: Voting Terms (10 questions)

    Question 29

    What are "Yeas" and "Nays"?

    Answer **Yeas** (or Ayes) are "yes" votes. **Nays** (or Noes) are "no" votes.

    Question 30

    How many votes are needed to pass a bill in the Senate?

    Answer **20 votes** (majority of 38 Senators)

    Question 31

    How many votes are needed for immediate effect?

    Answer **26 votes** (2/3 of 38 Senators)

    Question 32

    What is a voice vote?

    Answer A **voice vote** is when Senators collectively say "aye" or "no" without individual votes being recorded. The presiding officer determines the outcome based on volume.

    Question 33

    What is a roll call vote?

    Answer A **roll call vote** is when each Senator's individual vote is recorded. The Secretary calls each Senator's name and they respond "yes," "no," or "pass."

    Question 34

    What is a quorum?

    Answer A **quorum** is the minimum number of Senators required to conduct business: **20 of 38** in Michigan Senate.

    Question 35

    What does "Excused" mean in a vote count?

    Answer **Excused** means a Senator is absent with permission (illness, official duty). They are not counted in the vote total.

    Question 36

    What does "Not Voting" mean?

    Answer **Not Voting** means a Senator is present but chose not to vote (rare, sometimes due to conflict of interest).

    Question 37

    What is unanimous consent?

    Answer **Unanimous consent** is when something is done without a formal vote because no Senator objects. Used for routine or non-controversial actions.

    Question 38

    A bill passes 22-16, but immediate effect is 25-13. Is immediate effect granted?

    Answer **No, immediate effect is DENIED.** While 25 is more than the 20 needed for passage, immediate effect requires 26 votes. The bill still becomes law but takes effect 90 days after session ends, not immediately.

    Section 5: Floor Procedures (8 questions)

    Question 39

    What does "FA" stand for?

    Answer **Floor Amendment** - amendments offered by Senators during General Orders (e.g., FA 1, FA 2)

    Question 40

    Can amendments be offered during Third Reading?

    Answer Generally **no**. Third Reading is for final passage vote only. Amendments require special consent (unanimous or 2/3 vote), which is rare.

    Question 41

    What is a motion to advance?

    Answer A **motion to advance to Third Reading** moves a bill from General Orders to the Third Reading calendar after debate and amendments are complete.

    Question 42

    What does "motion to table" do?

    Answer **Motion to table** postpones action indefinitely, which usually kills the bill.

    Question 43

    What is a point of order?

    Answer A **point of order** is when a Senator believes Senate rules are being violated and asks the presiding officer to rule on whether the objection is valid.

    Question 44

    What is a motion to reconsider?

    Answer A **motion to reconsider** brings back a vote that already happened, nullifying the previous vote so a new vote can be taken. Usually must be made the same day or next session day.

    Question 45

    When does the five-day rule start?

    Answer When a bill is **placed on the Third Reading calendar**. The bill must be available for 5 calendar days before final passage vote.

    Question 46

    Are roll call votes required for passage on Third Reading?

    Answer **Yes.** Third Reading passage votes always require roll call votes (individual votes recorded).

    Section 6: House Interaction (6 questions)

    Question 47

    How many members are in the Michigan House of Representatives?

    Answer **110 Representatives**

    Question 48

    What does "concur" mean?

    Answer **Concur** means one chamber agrees to accept the other chamber's amendments to a bill.

    Question 49

    What does "non-concur" mean?

    Answer **Non-concur** means one chamber rejects the other chamber's amendments, usually leading to a Conference Committee.

    Question 50

    What is a Conference Committee?

    Answer A **Conference Committee** is a joint Senate-House committee (3 Senators + 3 Representatives) created to negotiate a compromise when chambers pass different versions of a bill.

    Question 51

    Can legislators amend a Conference Report?

    Answer **No.** Conference Reports must be voted up or down as written. No amendments are allowed.

    Question 52

    How many votes are needed for the Senate to concur in House amendments?

    Answer **20 votes** (same as for passage - simple majority of 38)

    Section 7: Special Terms and Situations (8 questions)

    Question 53

    What is immediate effect?

    Answer **Immediate effect** allows a law to take effect immediately upon the Governor's signature instead of waiting 90 days after session ends. Requires 2/3 vote (26 of 38).

    Question 54

    If a bill does NOT have immediate effect, when does it take effect?

    Answer **90 days after the Legislature adjourns for the year** (sine die adjournment).

    Question 55

    What does "sine die" mean?

    Answer **Sine die** (Latin: "without day") means final adjournment of the legislative session. Bills that haven't passed must be reintroduced in the next session.

    Question 56

    What is "lame duck" session?

    Answer The period between the November election and the end of the year (sine die), when some legislators who were defeated or are retiring are still serving.

    Question 57

    What is a Conference Report?

    Answer The **Conference Report** is the compromise version of a bill produced by a Conference Committee (3+3 members) to resolve differences between Senate and House versions.

    Question 58

    How many votes are needed to override a Governor's veto?

    Answer **2/3 vote in both chambers** (26 in Senate, 74 in House).

    Question 59

    What is a pocket veto?

    Answer A **pocket veto** occurs when the Governor takes no action on a bill and the Legislature has adjourned. The bill dies because it cannot be returned to the Legislature.

    Question 60

    Must both chambers pass identical versions of a bill?

    Answer **Yes.** Both the Senate and House must pass **exactly identical versions** before a bill can go to the Governor. This is constitutionally required.

    Scenario-Based Questions (5 questions)

    Scenario Question 1

    You hear: "Senate Bill 345 on General Orders. Senator Lee offers Floor Amendment 1. Voice vote. Amendment adopted. Senator Smith moves to advance to Third Reading. Motion carried."

    What happened?

    Answer - SB 345 was on General Orders - FA 1 offered by Sen. Lee: ADOPTED (voice vote) - Motion to advance to Third Reading: CARRIED - Bill is now on Third Reading calendar (five-day rule begins)

    Scenario Question 2

    You hear: "Third Reading of Senate Bill 456. Roll call: Yeas 19, Nays 19. The bill fails."

    Why did it fail?

    Answer The bill needed **20 votes** (majority of 38) to pass. It received only 19 votes, so it **failed**. (Note: If it were truly 19-19, the Lt. Governor could break the tie, but with 38 total, ties are actually rare. This would mean senators absent or not voting.)

    Scenario Question 3

    "Message from House: House passed Senate Bill 567 with amendments. Senator Martinez moves to concur. Roll call: Yeas 21, Nays 17. What happens next?"

    Answer The Senate **concurred** in the House amendments (21 votes, needs 20). Since both chambers have now passed identical versions, the bill is **enrolled and sent to the Governor**.

    Scenario Question 4

    "Senate Bill 789 passes 30-8. Immediate effect vote: 25-13. What is the status?"

    Answer - Bill **PASSED** (30 votes, needs 20) - Immediate effect **DENIED** (25 votes, needs 26) - Bill still becomes law but takes effect **90 days after sine die** (not immediately) - Bill sent to House for House process

    Scenario Question 5

    "The Committee on Education reports SB 234 with recommendation 'do pass as amended' and refers the bill to the Committee on Appropriations. Where does the bill go?"

    Answer The bill goes to the **Appropriations Committee** (NOT to General Orders). Multiple referrals mean the bill goes to the next committee first. Only after Appropriations reports the bill will it go to General Orders.
